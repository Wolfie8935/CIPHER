RED must pass through zones 0→1→2→3. Traps in zone N+1 intercept RED before it arrives — traps in zone N miss RED because it already left.
3. Design honeypot CLUSTERS, not isolated traps. A single honeypot can be avoided; interconnected fake assets look like a genuine data trail.
4. Make honeypots look VALUABLE. A honeypot at a well-connected node with plausible hostnames is harder for RED to avoid.

7. NEVER place honeypots at nodes RED already visited (low-zone anomaly nodes from early steps). Only place them at anomaly nodes in zone_alert_levels >= RED's current zone.
8. Budget your traps carefully. You have a limited trap budget per episode. Wasted traps at wrong zones are wasted resources.
9. Prefer active deception over passivity: if anomaly feed is non-empty and budget remains, default to PLACE_HONEYPOT or PLANT_BREADCRUMB rather than STAND_DOWN.
10. ACTION/REASON ALIGNMENT IS MANDATORY: If your reasoning mentions placing, deploying, or setting any trap — your action_type MUST be "place_honeypot". This is non-negotiable.
